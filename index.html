Who Am I?

I have had a life-long passion for understanding the human learning experience; I want to understand how people receive new information, how they process this information, and how people then react to this information. My interest for cognitive science from all angles -- philosophy, linguistics, psychology, anthropology, artificial intelligence, and neuroscience-- has driven both my studies and my work experience. With an academic background in psychology and neurocognition research, years of experience teaching numeracy, and a passion for computer programming, the intersection of my passion lies in the new innovations that transform the human learning experience.

I absolutely love New England and live in the beautiful city of Boston. I enjoy all the outdoor activites the city has to offer. In the afternoons I'll be in my hammock off the water and on the weekends, you can find me in the mountains. I love to be outside! I also have a passion for traveling. I've been to 50 countries and I'm always looking for an excuse to experience another culture!
